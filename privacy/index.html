Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 14, 2026 · Last updated: April 14, 2026

1. Overview

Stickerpunk is designed with your privacy as a default. The App does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data on any server operated by the Developer. Your photos never leave your device — all sticker creation happens entirely on-device.

Limited data is collected by RevenueCat solely to manage your in-app subscription, as described in Section 3 below.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

The Developer does not collect:

  • Your name, email address, or any account information.
  • Your photos, images, or any stickers you create — these are processed on-device and never transmitted to the Developer or any Developer-operated server.
  • Your location or GPS data.
  • Your contacts or address book.
  • Usage analytics or in-app behavior events.
  • Crash logs or diagnostic reports (beyond what Apple’s iOS may collect at the OS level, which is governed by Apple’s own privacy policy).

3. Information Collected by Third Parties

RevenueCat

The App uses RevenueCat (RevenueCat, Inc.) to manage in-app subscriptions and purchases. To verify and process subscription status, RevenueCat may collect:

  • A pseudonymous app user ID generated at install time.
  • Purchase receipts and transaction data provided by the App Store.
  • Device information such as device type, operating system version, and locale.

No name, email address, or payment card information is shared with RevenueCat — all payment processing is handled entirely by Apple. RevenueCat’s data practices are described in their privacy policy: https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy.

Telegram Messenger

When you choose to export a sticker to Telegram, you are interacting with the Telegram application. Any data handled by Telegram at that point is governed solely by Telegram’s Privacy Policy. The Developer has no access to data handled by Telegram and is not affiliated with Telegram.

4. On-Device Photo Processing

All photo selection and sticker generation occurs entirely on your device using Apple’s on-device frameworks and APIs. No photo, image, or derived data is transmitted to any server operated by the Developer at any time. The Developer has no ability to access, view, or retrieve any photos you use with the App.

5. Children’s Privacy (COPPA)

Stickerpunk is not directed at children under the age of 13, and the Developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. In the event that the Developer becomes aware that personal information has been collected from a child under 13, it will be promptly deleted.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child under 13 has provided personal information through the App, please contact the Developer at sashkn2@gmail.com.

6. California Residents — CCPA Rights

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) may provide you with certain rights regarding personal information:

  • Right to Know: You have the right to request information about the personal information collected about you. As described above, the Developer does not collect or store personal information. Any personal information collected by RevenueCat is subject to RevenueCat’s own CCPA practices — please contact RevenueCat directly or visit RevenueCat’s privacy policy.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request deletion of your personal information. Because the Developer does not collect or store personal data, there is nothing for the Developer to delete on your behalf.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale: The Developer does not sell personal information to third parties.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: You will not receive discriminatory treatment for exercising any of your CCPA rights.

7. Data Retention

The Developer does not retain any personal data. Any data retained by RevenueCat is subject to RevenueCat’s own data retention policies, available at https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy.

8. Security

Because no personal data is transmitted to or stored by the Developer, there are no Developer-operated servers or databases that require securing. On-device data is protected by iOS device security, including device encryption and application sandboxing enforced by the operating system.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

The Developer may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted at this URL with a revised “Last updated” date. Your continued use of the App after the effective date of any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
